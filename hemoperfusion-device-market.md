<p><p>The hemoperfusion device market is primarily divided into two types: Activated Carbon Adsorption Hemoperfusion Devices and Resin Adsorption Hemoperfusion Devices. Activated carbon devices utilize granular activated carbon to adsorb toxins from the blood, effectively clearing harmful substances. In contrast, resin adsorption devices use synthetic polymers that specifically bind to various toxins, offering targeted treatment for specific conditions. Both types play crucial roles in managing toxin-related disorders, enhancing blood purification processes and improving patient outcomes in critical care settings.</p></p>

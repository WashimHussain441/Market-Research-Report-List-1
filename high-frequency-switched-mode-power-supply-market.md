<p><p>The high-frequency switched-mode power supply (SMPS) market features two primary control types: voltage mode control and current mode control. Voltage mode control regulates output voltage by adjusting the duty cycle of the switching element, offering simplicity and stability. In contrast, current mode control monitors output current, enhancing transient response and providing inherent overcurrent protection. Both methods cater to diverse applications, with voltage mode popular for its straightforward design and current mode favored for improved performance in dynamic load conditions.</p></p>

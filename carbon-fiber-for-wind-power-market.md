<p><p>The carbon fiber market for wind power is categorized into several types based on their raw materials. PAN-based carbon fiber, derived from polyacrylonitrile, offers high tensile strength and is widely used in turbine blades due to its superior mechanical properties. Viscose-based carbon fiber, made from regenerated cellulose, provides good thermal stability and is cost-effective. Pitch-based carbon fiber, sourced from petroleum or coal tar, is known for its high thermal conductivity and strength. Other types may include blends or specialty fibers tailored for specific applications in the wind power industry.</p></p>

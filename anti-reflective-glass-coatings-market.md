<p><p>Anti-reflective glass coatings enhance light transmission by minimizing reflections. The market primarily features two types of deposition methods: electron beam evaporation and sputtering. </p><p>Electron beam evaporation involves heating targets to produce vapor that condenses on the glass surface, creating a uniform coating. In contrast, sputtering uses a plasma to eject material from a target, depositing it onto the substrate. Both methods provide high-quality coatings, with electron beam evaporation offering precision while sputtering is favored for its versatility and scalability in production.</p></p>

<p><p>The rail battery systems market comprises three primary types: Lead-Acid, Nickel-Cadmium, and Lithium-Ion. Lead-Acid batteries are cost-effective and widely used, offering reliable performance but limited lifespan. Nickel-Cadmium batteries provide robust performance in harsh conditions and have a longer cycle life, though they are heavier and more expensive. Lithium-Ion batteries are gaining popularity due to their high energy density, lightweight design, and longer operational life, making them ideal for modern rail applications focused on efficiency and sustainability.</p></p>

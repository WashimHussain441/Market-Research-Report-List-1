<p><p>The oilfield surfactants market encompasses various types, each serving unique functions. Anionic surfactants, characterized by their negatively charged particles, effectively reduce surface tension and enhance oil recovery. Non-ionic surfactants, devoid of charge, offer excellent stability and are versatile in various applications. Cationic surfactants, with positive charges, provide antimicrobial properties and benefit drilling fluids. Amphoteric surfactants can function as both anionic and cationic, adapting to diverse conditions, making them valuable in enhancing efficiency and performance in oilfield operations.</p></p>

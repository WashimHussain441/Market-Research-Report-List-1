<p><p>The automotive aluminum alloy wheel market comprises primarily three types: casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into a mold, creating complex shapes at lower costs, ideal for mass production. Forging involves shaping heated aluminum under pressure, resulting in stronger and lighter wheels, preferred for high-performance vehicles. Other methods may include spinning or machining, offering unique designs and lightweight solutions. Each method balances cost, performance, and aesthetic appeal, catering to various automotive segments and consumer preferences.</p></p>

<p><p>The Photovoltaic Grade Trichlorosilane market comprises two main production processes: Direct Chlorination (DC) and Hydrochlorination (HC). The DC process involves the direct reaction of silicon with chlorine gas, resulting in high purity trichlorosilane suitable for photovoltaic applications. In contrast, the HC process utilizes silicon and hydrochloric acid, producing trichlorosilane through a less direct method, often leading to different purity levels. Both methods cater to the increasing demand for high-quality materials in solar cell manufacturing.</p></p>

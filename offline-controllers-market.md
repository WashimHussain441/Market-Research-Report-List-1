<p><ul><li>Current Mode</li><li>Voltage Mode</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Offline controllers are categorized into two main market types: Current Mode and Voltage Mode. </p><p>Current Mode controllers regulate output by adjusting the duty cycle based on the load current, ensuring rapid response to changes and improved stability. They are often used in applications requiring high efficiency and precise control. </p><p>Voltage Mode controllers, on the other hand, adjust the output by varying the voltage to maintain the desired level. These controllers are simple to implement and suitable for low-complexity designs. Both types play crucial roles in power supply solutions.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Chemical Filters</li><li>Physical Filters</li></ul></p>
<p><p>UV filters in the personal care market are categorized into chemical and physical filters. Chemical filters absorb UV radiation, converting it into heat, which is then released from the skin. They tend to be lightweight and can provide broad-spectrum protection. In contrast, physical filters, such as zinc oxide or titanium dioxide, create a barrier on the skin's surface that reflects and scatters UV rays. Both types are essential for sun protection in skincare products, catering to diverse consumer needs and preferences.</p></p>

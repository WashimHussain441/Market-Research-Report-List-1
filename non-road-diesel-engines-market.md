<p><p>The non-road diesel engines market comprises three primary types based on their cooling methods: water-cooled, air-cooled, and oil-cooled engines. Water-cooled engines utilize a liquid coolant to maintain optimal operating temperatures, providing efficient thermal regulation, making them suitable for heavy-duty applications. Air-cooled engines rely on air circulation for cooling, offering simplicity and lighter designs, often used in portable equipment. Oil-cooled engines employ oil for cooling, ensuring effective heat dissipation, typically found in specialized machinery. Each type serves distinct operational needs and environments.</p></p>

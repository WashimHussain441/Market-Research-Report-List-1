<p><p>Hydrogen generators for the green energy market primarily consist of three technologies: Traditional Alkaline Electrolysis, PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) Electrolysis, and Solid Oxide Electrolysis. Traditional Alkaline Electrolysis uses an alkaline electrolyte to split water, offering cost-effectiveness and reliability. PEM Electrolysis utilizes a solid polymer membrane, enabling higher efficiency and dynamic operation, suitable for fluctuating renewable energy sources. Solid Oxide Electrolysis operates at high temperatures, enhancing efficiency by utilizing heat, making it ideal for industrial applications that integrate with waste heat recovery systems.</p></p>

Fluoropolymer valves, known for their excellent chemical resistance and low permeability, are ideal for handling aggressive fluids in semiconductor applications. In contrast, stainless steel valves offer durability and high-temperature resistance, making them suitable for more robust environments. Both types maintain the stringent purity requirements essential for semiconductor manufacturing, ensuring minimal contamination and optimal performance in critical processes.</p></p>

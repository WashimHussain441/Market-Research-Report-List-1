<p><ul><li>Dry Process</li><li>Wet Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>HMPE (High Modulus Polyethylene) fibers are produced using two main processes: dry and wet. The dry process involves spinning HMPE fibers from a solid polymer, followed by drawing to enhance strength and modulus. This method is often used for lightweight applications. In contrast, the wet process dissolves the polymer in a solvent before spinning, allowing for finer fiber production and improved properties, making it suitable for high-performance applications in fields like aerospace, military, and industrial textiles.</p></p>

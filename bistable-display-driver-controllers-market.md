<p><p>Bistable display driver controllers encompass two primary types: dot-matrix bistable display integrated circuits (ICs) and segment bistable display driver ICs. Dot-matrix bistable displays consist of an array of pixels that can be individually controlled, making them suitable for complex graphics and text. In contrast, segment bistable display driver ICs manage fixed segments (like digits) in lower-resolution applications, ideal for simple alphanumeric displays. Both types leverage low-power bistable technology for efficient performance and persistent image retention.</p></p>

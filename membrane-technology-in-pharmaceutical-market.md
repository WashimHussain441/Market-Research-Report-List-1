<p><p>Membrane technology in the pharmaceutical market involves separation processes using semi-permeable membranes to purify, concentrate, or fractionate substances. **Microfiltration** removes particles and microorganisms, ideal for sterilization. **Ultrafiltration** separates macromolecules and proteins, enabling concentration and purification. **Nanofiltration** targets smaller molecules, removing divalent ions and organic compounds. **Reverse Osmosis** eliminates dissolved salts and impurities, producing high-purity water. **Other** technologies include dialysis and electrodialysis, catering to specialized pharmaceutical needs. These methods enhance drug formulation, water purification, and bioprocessing, ensuring safety, efficiency, and regulatory compliance.</p></p>

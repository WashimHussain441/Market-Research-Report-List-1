<p><ul><li>Cationic Softeners</li><li>Anionic Softeners</li><li>Non-Ionic Softeners</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The textile reactive softeners market primarily comprises three types: cationic, anionic, and non-ionic softeners. Cationic softeners are positively charged and effectively bond with negatively charged fabric fibers, enhancing softness and providing anti-static properties. Anionic softeners, negatively charged, are used for specific fabrics to improve handle and reduce fabric stiffness. Non-ionic softeners, with no charge, offer versatility across various textiles without altering dyes, making them suitable for delicate fabrics. Each type plays a crucial role in achieving desired textile finishes.</p></p>

<p><p>Looms without shuttles, such as rapier, air-jet, water-jet, and tissue looms, are advanced weaving machines that enhance efficiency and versatility. Rapier looms utilize a pair of gripping elements to carry the weft yarn across the width of the fabric. Air-jet looms use a jet of air to propel the weft, offering high-speed weaving. Water-jet looms employ a stream of water to transport the yarn, ideal for synthetic fibers. Tissue looms are specialized for producing lightweight fabrics, emphasizing speed and precision.</p></p>
